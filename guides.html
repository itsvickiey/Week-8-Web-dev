Expert Care Guides

The Essential Guide to Watering

Watering is the most common pitfall for new plant parents. The key is consistency, not frequency. Always check the top two inches of soil before watering.

Advanced Tip: Most tropical plants prefer deep, infrequent watering. Water until it drains from the bottom hole, then discard any excess water in the saucer after 30 minutes to prevent root rot. Succulents, on the other hand, require almost no water during winter dormancy.

Use filtered or distilled water if your tap water is heavily chlorinated, as this can affect sensitive plant roots over time.

Choosing the Right Light

Understanding light is crucial. 'Bright, indirect light' is the gold standard, meaning the plant can see the sky but the sun's rays aren't directly hitting its leaves.
